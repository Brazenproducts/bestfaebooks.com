#3

The Cruel Prince

by Holly Black — The Folk of the Air, Book 1
★★★★★

Holly Black is one of the genre's defining voices, and The Cruel Prince is arguably her masterpiece. Jude is a mortal living in the High Court of Faerie, raised by the fae general who murdered her parents — and desperately trying to carve out a place of power in a world that sees her as beneath notice.

The political intrigue here is sharper than almost anything else in the genre. The enemies-to-lovers dynamic between Jude and Prince Cardan is one of the most psychologically complex in fae fiction. Dark, clever, and deeply satisfying.

📚 Fae Fantasy Buying Guide 2025

Not all fae fantasy is the same. Here's a quick guide to help you find your ideal starting point.

Dark Faerie Courts

For pure court intrigue and dark fae atmosphere, start with The Cruel Prince (Holly Black) or Bound by Thorns (Fern Allern). Both focus on mortal characters navigating dangerous fae politics.

Epic Romantasy

If you want fae fantasy that's heavily romance-driven with steamy content, ACOTAR and From Blood and Ash are your go-tos. Both have massive fanbases and long series to keep you reading.

Best for Beginners

New to fae fantasy? Start with A Court of Thorns and Roses. It's the most accessible, best-paced introduction to the genre and sets up most of what makes fae fantasy compelling.

Kindle Unlimited Tips

Sarah J. Maas books are traditionally published and not in KU. Independent and hybrid authors like Jennifer L. Armentrout sometimes place books in KU. Always check current availability before buying.

❓ FAQ

What is fae fantasy?
Fae fantasy is a fantasy subgenre featuring the fae — magical, often morally ambiguous beings from folklore who operate by their own ancient rules. Modern fae fantasy typically involves mortal characters crossing into or interacting with fae courts and worlds, often with romantic elements.
Is fae fantasy the same as romantasy?
Romantasy is a broader term for fantasy with romance as a central element. Fae fantasy is one of the most popular subgenres of romantasy, but not all fae fantasy is romantasy — some focuses more purely on the fantasy plot. The books on this list tend toward the romantasy end.
What reading level are these books?
Most books on this list are written for adult readers (18+) and contain mature content. The Cruel Prince is sometimes categorized as YA crossover but deals with adult themes. Always check individual content warnings before reading or recommending to younger readers.
Which series has the most books to binge?
The Throne of Glass series has 8 main books plus novellas. The ACOTAR series has 5 main books. From Blood and Ash (Blood and Ash series) currently has 6 books. All are worth the long commitment.
